How to Read This Dashboard

Quick Cheat Sheet
  • — Daily PF above 1.00 + green Runs Delta + HR Factor above 1.00 + warm weather + wind blowing out
  • — Daily PF below 0.95 + red Runs Delta + HR Factor below 0.95 + cold weather + wind blowing in
  • — HR Factor above 1.05 + positive LU HR Diff + confirmed lineups (green checkmarks)
  • — Dashes in lineup columns, "est." in weather, or dashes in Win%/Edge — data isn't ready, check back later

Matchup

What it is
The two teams playing and whether we know their actual batting lineups yet.
What to look for
Green checkmark = real lineup posted by the team. Dash = we're still guessing. "DOME" tag = indoor game, weather doesn't matter for that one.
Example
"If you see dashes on both sides, the park factor numbers are solid but the Lineup HR Diff column is a guess — wait for checkmarks before using that one."

Pitchers

What it is
Who's starting on the mound for each side. Home pitcher listed first.
What to look for
This is context, not a signal by itself. But a flyball pitcher at a wind-blowing-out park is a different story than a groundball pitcher at the same park.
Example
"If you see a known flyball guy pitching at Wrigley and the wind column shows +12, that's a HR party waiting to happen."

Daily PF (Park Factor)

What it is
Is this ballpark playing hitter-friendly or pitcher-friendly TODAY? Think of 1.000 as perfectly neutral. Higher means more runs expected. Lower means fewer runs.
What to look for
Above 1.02 is noticeably hitter-friendly. Below 0.95 is noticeably pitcher-friendly. Most games land between 0.93 and 1.08.
Example
"Coors Field almost always shows high here. But Petco Park on a cold night might show 0.88 — that's a graveyard for offense."

Runs Delta — The Money Column

What it is
How much is TODAY'S weather pushing scoring away from what the market already expects at this park? The betting market already knows Coors is a hitter's park. What it doesn't know is whether TODAY at Coors is even more hitter-friendly than usual, or less. That gap is where the edge lives.
What to look for
Green/positive = weather is boosting runs ABOVE what the market prices in. Red/negative = weather is suppressing runs BELOW expectations. The bigger the number, the bigger the potential edge.
Example
"Wrigley has a seasonal park factor of 1.04. Today it shows +0.06 delta because it's 85 degrees with wind blowing out. The market set the total based on 'Wrigley is slightly hitter-friendly.' The weather today says it's WAY more hitter-friendly than that. That gap is where you find over/under value."

HR Factor

What it is
Same idea as Daily PF but specifically for home runs. How much does today's venue + weather help or hurt the long ball?
What to look for
Above 1.05 = home runs are flying today. Below 0.90 = the ball is dying at the warning track. This is your HR prop column.
Example
"HR Factor at 1.12 + a power hitter in the lineup = look at his HR prop. HR Factor at 0.85 = fade HR props, take strikeout props instead."

LU HR Diff (Lineup HR Difference)

What it is
Does the home team's batting lineup have more home run power AT THIS SPECIFIC PARK than the visiting lineup? This isn't just "who has more power" — it's "whose power matches this park's shape."
What to look for
Big positive = home lineup's hitters are built for this park. Big negative = visitors have the power advantage. Near zero = roughly even.
Example
"A team full of left-handed pull hitters at Yankee Stadium (short right field) will show a huge advantage here. That same lineup at Fenway (deep right, short left) might show the opposite. It's not about who hits harder — it's about who hits harder IN THIS PARK."

SP Tunnel (Pitch Tunneling Score)

What it is
How well each starter disguises different pitches at the batter's decision point (23.77 ft from home plate). Two pitches that look identical there but end up in different locations cause decision errors. Format: Home / Away, rolling average of last 5 starts.
What to look for
Higher = more deception. Typical range ~1.30–1.50. Pitcher with noticeably higher tunnel score has a stuff advantage over the opposing batters on this slate that isn't priced into velocity or spin.
Example
"Corbin shows 1.456 tonight vs Castillo at 1.371. Corbin's fastball and slider look the same coming out of his hand, making his 88 mph fastball play up. Elite tunnelers beat their Stuff+ rating by 10–20% in whiff rate."

F5 Exit Risk (First-5 Early Exit)

What it is
Modeled probability that the starter fails to complete 5 innings, based on pitch efficiency, walk rate, and recent stamina. Format: Home / Away. Feeds F5 totals and runline bets directly.
What to look for
Below 0.03 = extremely durable (green). Above 0.10 = real risk (red) — bullpen gets exposed in inning 4–5. Big gap between home and away suggests F5 value.
Example
"Sean Burke at 0.321 means the model thinks there's a 32% chance he doesn't reach the 5th. If the market is pricing F5 based on his season line, the over on F5 total runs has hidden value because the bullpen is coming in early."

Win% / Edge / Rec

What it is
Our model's pre-game prediction. Win% is the home team's chance of winning. Edge is how much our number disagrees with the betting line. Rec tells you whether the edge is big enough to bet.
What to look for
"BET" in green with a tier badge = we found value. "PASS" = not enough edge. Dashes = model hasn't scored this game yet (check back later).
Example
"Win% shows 58% home, the book has them at -130 (implied 56.5%). Our edge is 1.5% on the home side. If the Rec says BET, our confidence system says that 1.5% is real and big enough to act on."

Weather (T / W / H)

What it is
Temperature, wind direction, and humidity at game time. These are the raw ingredients that drive the park factor numbers above.
What to look for
T (temp): Hot = more offense. Cold = less. Above 72°F the ball starts carrying noticeably better.
W (wind): Positive = blowing OUT toward the outfield (helps HR, more runs). Negative = blowing IN (kills HR, fewer runs). Already adjusted for which direction center field faces.
H (humidity): Higher humidity actually means slightly MORE carry — water vapor is lighter than dry air. Small effect compared to temp and wind.
"est." prefix = we don't have real weather data yet. These are placeholder numbers. Don't bet based on estimated weather.
Example
"82°F, +11 wind, 45% humidity at Wrigley = absolute launch pad. 48°F, -7 wind, 80% humidity at Guaranteed Rate = bring a sleeping bag, nothing is leaving the yard."
